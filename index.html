Why we exist

Our mission

Our mission empowers middle and high school students to build lifelong healthy habits through evidence-based education, injury prevention, nutrition, movement, and community service.

Injury Prevention

Warm-ups, movement mechanics, and habits that keep young athletes on the field and off the sidelines.

Nutrition & Hydration

Practical, age-appropriate fueling and hydration habits that support growth, energy, and performance.

Sleep & Recovery

Recovery science made simple — why rest is training, and how to build routines that stick.

Healthy Athletic Habits

Balancing academics, sport, and rest — the everyday habits that make performance sustainable.

Play Strong, Stay Strong

Workshop Series

A free wellness series for middle school athletes

Play Strong, Stay Strong brings athletic trainers, physical therapists, and nutrition professionals directly to student athletes — with a student team handling everything from outreach to on-the-ground demos.

  • 01 Injury prevention and warm-ups
  • 02 Mobility and recovery
  • 03 Hydration and nutrition
  • 04 Sleep and healthy routines
  • 05 Balancing academics and sports
How it works

Professionals lead the medical and technical content — athletic trainers, PTs, and nutrition experts volunteer their expertise.

Students organize — our team handles scheduling, promotion, expert interviews, and student-friendly materials.

Athletes leave with real tools — simple routines they can actually use in season, not just information.

Founder Story

Started by an athlete, for athletes

D

Desmond

Founder, Bellevue Youth Wellness Initiative

Desmond founded the Bellevue Youth Wellness Initiative after seeing the same gap show up again and again: young athletes are pushed to train hard, but rarely taught how to take care of their bodies while they do it.

That gap became personal through his own experience competing in fencing and basketball, where he learned firsthand how much injury prevention, recovery, and nutrition shape whether an athlete keeps improving or keeps getting hurt. Time spent in a physical therapy clinic showed him what evidence-based care actually looks like up close, and his work with T-BUG and the National Youth Leadership Movement (NLYM) gave him a model for organizing student-led service that actually reaches the people it's meant for.

Play Strong, Stay Strong is Desmond's attempt to put all of that in one place: real expertise from professionals, translated by students, for the middle schoolers who need it most.
